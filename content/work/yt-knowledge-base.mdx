## The Hook
I watch about 10 hours of technical YouTube content a week. By Friday, I've forgotten 90% of the specific hacks I saw. I needed a brain that doesn't leak.

## The Problem
Information overload. YouTube is the world's best university, but it's ephemeral. You can't `CTRL+F` a video in your head. I was wasting hours re-watching videos just to find one line of code or one specific n8n node configuration.

## The Villain
The "Watch Later" Graveyard. We all have it. It's where good information goes to die.

## The Build
A pipeline that turns passive watching into an active, searchable knowledge base.

1. **Trigger**: Whenever I "Like" a video on YouTube.
2. **Extraction**: n8n pulls the video metadata and the auto-generated transcript.
3. **Synthesis**: Claude summarizes the transcript into a "Cheat Sheet" format:
   - The Core Problem.
   - The Solution Steps.
   - Specific Tools/Libraries mentioned.
   - Code snippets or Workflow JSON if applicable.
4. **Delivery**: The summary is formatted into a `.md` file and synced directly to my **Obsidian** vault via a local file server.

## The Stack
- **n8n**: Scheduled polling of the YouTube API.
- **Claude**: Summarizing 40-minute transcripts into 200-word punchy summaries.
- **Obsidian**: The final destination for all my thoughts.

## The Outcome
- A "Second Brain" that grows automatically.
- Searchable database of every technical concept I've consumed in the last 6 months.
- **Research time cut by 50%** when starting new projects.

## What I'd Do Differently
The auto-generated transcripts are often full of errors, especially for technical terms. I eventually added a "Glossary" step to the Claude prompt to force-correct common misspellings (e.g., "n-eight-n" → "n8n").
