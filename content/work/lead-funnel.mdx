## The Hook
400+ leads per day. One sales person. Zero triage. My client wasn't just losing money; they were losing their mind. 

## The Problem
The business was scaling faster than their operations could handle. Every morning, the sales team faced a mountain of emails, LinkedIn DMs, and form submissions. 80% of these were "junk" or low-intent, but finding the 20% "gold" required manually clicking through every profile.

## The Villain
The Manual Process. It's the silent killer of scale. Human bottlenecking was preventing the sales team from doing what they do best: selling. Instead, they were data entry clerks.

## The Build
I built an automated triage system that works while they sleep.

1. **Ingestion**: An n8n workflow monitors all lead sources (Webhooks, Gmail, LinkedIn).
2. **Enrichment**: The system scrapes the lead's company website and LinkedIn profile.
3. **Scoring**: I fed this raw data into **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** with a custom scoring rubric. Claude evaluates the lead based on company size, industry, and the specific pain point described.
4. **Action**: 
   - **Tier A (Hot)**: Immediate Slack alert to the founder and a drafted email in Outlook.
   - **Tier B (Follow-up)**: Added to a Supabase CRM for daily review.
   - **Tier C (Junk)**: Auto-archived with a polite "not a fit" auto-response.

## The Stack
- **n8n**: The glue. Flexible enough to handle complex logic without the overhead of a full backend.
- **Claude API**: The brain. Its ability to follow nuanced scoring rubrics is far superior to standard GPT-4 for this use case.
- **Supabase**: The memory. Used for deduplication and logging.

## The Outcome
- **80% reduction** in manual review time.
- **Zero** hot leads missed.
- The sales team now starts their day with a list of the top 10 people to call, instead of 400 emails to read.

## What I'd Do Differently
I'd implement a "human-in-the-loop" feedback loop earlier. For the first 48 hours, Claude was a bit too aggressive with Tier C. Adding a simple Slack "Approve/Reject" button for the first 100 leads would have trained the prompt faster.
