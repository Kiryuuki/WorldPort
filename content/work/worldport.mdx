## The Hook
The web is becoming generic. Templates, UI kits, and "best practices" have turned the internet into a beige office building. I wanted a galaxy.

## The Problem
A personal website should be a signal of how you think, not just a list of what you've done. Most portfolios are static resumes. They don't feel "alive." They don't show the craft behind the code.

## The Villain
The Generic Template. It hides the person behind the "Solution Architect" title. 

## The Build
I built WorldPort to be an experience, not just a page.

1. **The Aesthetic**: Deep space. `#010611`. It represents the solo operator—floating, building, signaling.
2. **The Earth**: A Three.js scene that anchors the page. It's not just a decoration; it's scroll-linked. As you move through my story, the world turns.
3. **The Cursor**: An asteroid particle system. It's a small touch that reminds the visitor they are in a custom-built environment.
4. **The Content**: Driven by MDX. Storytelling first. No buzzwords.

## The Stack
- **Next.js 15**: Bleeding edge. Using the App Router and React Server Components for maximum performance.
- **Three.js**: Bringing 3D to the web without the bloat.
- **GSAP + Lenis**: The gold standard for web animations and smooth scrolling.
- **Tailwind CSS v4**: For ultra-fast styling with modern CSS features.

## The Outcome
- A site that people actually want to explore.
- A clear demonstration of my ability to blend high-end design with robust technical implementation.
- **Signal generated.**

## What I'd Do Differently
I spent too much time trying to download 8K textures from Wikimedia and getting rate-limited. Next time, I'll host my assets on a dedicated CDN from day one. (Meta: This is happening right now).
